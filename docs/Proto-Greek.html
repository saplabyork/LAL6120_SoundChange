Approach

Our goal is to induce an acoustic scenario for the listener such that the signal is obscured. How do we do this? With noise of various types. We then embed the signal (our kw sounds) in the noise and ask listeners what they hear. Do they hear a ‚Äúk‚Äù, ‚Äúp,‚Äù ‚Äút‚Äù? What‚Äôs the best way to ask this?

The signal

The Greek data show that kw- changes to p or t depending on the following vowel: kwi and kwe > ti and te; kwu and kwo > pu and po. So we need to record signal stimuli that encompass all of the appropriate vowel contexts. Do we do this using monosyllables or words (in English)? What are the advantages/disadvantages of both?

LAL6120-Outline/Schedule

LAL 6120: Phonological and Phonetic Analysis

Sound Change

Instructor: Chandan Narayan
Course time: Wednesdays 11:30-3:30pm, Ross S803

Outline

Seminar description:

This graduate seminar will be a rigourous introduction to the topic of sound change. We will survey the intellectual history of formal approaches in the sound change literature. The course presupposes your having some background exposure to basic principles in phonetics (articulatory and acoustic) and phonology (nothing overly theoretical). We will focus on acoustics and speech perception explanations in sound change, but also consider sociological and extra-linguistic forces affecting change. While much of the sound change literature (especially pre-1960s) sought to describe the what of sound change, I want our focus to be on the why (and to some extent how) of sound change.

Students will be required to present readings and lead discussion. Throughout the course we will be working on a collaborative sound change project

Seminar prerequisites:

This seminar is for first year graduate students in LAL. To that extent, the readings and discussion (and ultimately the work you produce) will reflect your background in Lingusitics, and phonetics and phonology in particular. I request that you contact me immediately if you do not have much background (i.e., intro courses in P&P), in which case we’ll have some work to do outside of class.

Learning outcomes:

  1. Demonstrate knowledge of theoretical approaches to sound change
  2. Demonstrate the ability to sketch out a theoretically motivated approach to your own problem in sound change
  3. Demonstrate ability to work within a team on a speech perception and acoustics project

Evaluation

Your grade in the course will be determined by your performance on the following:

  • 50% in class participation (including presenting readings)
    • Prepare outline handouts for readings with discussion questions
  • 50% contribution to collaborative class project

Course text and readings

The readings will be provided in pdf form under the Readings tab. In some cases we may have to read texts where there isn’t an electronic copy available. Library links will be provided in those cases.

Class structure

We will go over the week’s papers. A student assigned to that particular week will present a detailed summary of the articles (in a handout) along with a few discussion questions for us. We’ll also spend some time outlining strategies for our collaborative class project. On some occasions we’ll take field trips to the Phonetics Studio and the SAP Lab.
